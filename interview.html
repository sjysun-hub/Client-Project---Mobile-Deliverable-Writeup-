Interview Skills

Never go into an Interview Unprepared!

What is a Typical Interview?

  • 30-45 minute pitch of you
    • Demonstrate you can do the job!
    • Show the employer the value add you'd bring to the company
  • Opportunity for you to assess whether or not you'd want to work at the organization/with these people/in this role

Typical Interview Format

  • Introduction
  • Discussion (Q&A)
  • Questions you want to ask
  • The close (timeline & next steps)
  • Thank you/follow-up

What You Need to be Aware of

  • You need to know what you want
  • Confident/assertive behavior is valued by U.S. employers (attire, eye contact and body language are all important)
  • Decisions are made early!
  • Interviewers may have biases they bring to the interview

Interviews Are Also for You to Assess the Company!

Feel empowered to ask thoughtful questions that will help you determine if this is somewhere you'd like to work!

  • CDO Interview Protocol
  • 51 Questions to Ask in an Interview
  • Questions to Identify Inclusive Companies
  • Use sites like Glassdoor, Chezie and more to see if employee experiences match what they share

Sample 30-minute Case Outline

  • 1-5 Minutes
    • State the problem
    • Make observations
    • Ask clarifying questions
    • Develop a hypothesis
    • Construct the necessary framework
  • 5-25 Minutes
    • Gather the data
    • Perform analysis on the data and the information received from clarifying questions
    • Crunch any numbers that were given
    • Form necessary conclusions and recommendations
  • 25-30 Minutes
    • Collect final thoughts
    • Summarize recommendations to interviewer
    • Always include recommendations + risks
